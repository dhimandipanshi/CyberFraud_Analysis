# Cyber Fraud Analysis — Canada (2021–2025)

---

## Executive Summary

This project analyzed **295,000+ cyber fraud records (2021–2025)** from the Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre to understand national fraud trends, financial impact, and high-risk groups. The analysis revealed **$637M+ in total losses**, with fraud impact heavily concentrated in specific categories rather than evenly distributed across all incidents.

**Key Finding:** Investment scams dominate total financial losses despite not being the most frequent fraud type, making them the highest-impact target for prevention efforts. Ontario and Quebec, along with the 30–59 age group, represent the highest-risk segments.

---

## Business Problem

Cyber fraud in Canada is rising rapidly, but prevention resources are limited and currently distributed across all fraud types and regions without prioritization.

This creates a critical gap:

> Where should Canada focus enforcement and awareness efforts to reduce the highest financial losses from fraud?

The goal was to identify the fraud types, regions, and demographics causing the greatest financial harm to improve resource allocation and prevention effectiveness.

---

## Methodology

- Collected and analyzed **295,000+ fraud records (2021–2025)** from CAFC  
- Performed **data cleaning and standardization using Excel Power Query (20+ fields)**  
- Built an ETL pipeline for structured, analysis-ready dataset  
- Conducted exploratory analysis across fraud type, geography, and demographics  
- Performed trend and concentration analysis on financial losses  
- Developed an interactive **Power BI dashboard** for visualization and monitoring

## Key Results & Business Recommendations

- Total losses analyzed: **$637M+ across 295,000+ victims**
- Investment scams are the **largest driver of financial loss**
- Ontario and Quebec account for the **majority of fraud exposure**
- Age group **30–59 is the most affected and highest-value target**

**Core Insight:** Fraud impact is highly concentrated — a small number of fraud types and demographics drive most financial damage.

**Recommendation:** Prioritize investment scam prevention and focus interventions on high-risk regions (ON, QC) and working-age adults (30–59) for maximum impact.

---

## Recommended Actions

- Launch targeted **investment scam awareness campaigns** nationally  
- Focus enforcement and education efforts in **Ontario and Quebec**  
- Develop fraud prevention programs for **30–59 age group**  
- Strengthen early detection systems for high-loss fraud categories  
- Improve cross-institution collaboration for fraud monitoring
